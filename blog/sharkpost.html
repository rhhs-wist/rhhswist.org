STEM "Shark Tank"

November 8, 2023

We did an activity in which teams came up with ideas for and then pitched a STEM innovation which would then be judged and rated by the meeting attendees. The prompt for the activity was to decide on an environmental problem and then devise a STEM solution for it. The teams had to work on building connections STEM ideas and creations with sustainability. Each team had 10 minutes to research and prepare before getting 1 and a half minutes to present.

The first team, Team Robo, presented an idea to help with combatting the harmful effects of pesticides on plants and crops. Their idea was to create a nanorobot that could easily be spread over plants which would then repair the plants that had been harmed by pesticides and prevent any future harm from pesticides affecting these plants.

The second team, Team Pretty Princess, focused on addressing the large amounts of litter in the dead portions of the ocean. They proposed a robot vacuum that would be dropped into dead areas and use AI to navigate to areas with trash in them and suck in the trash. It would then filter out and purify the water and compress and store trash which could then be picked up and properly disposed of.

The third team, Team SS, centered on the loss of biodiversity and the sustainability of habitats. They suggested using genetic cloning to increase the populations of the animals that are currently endangered and nearing extinction. They would also use genetic cloning to create new species that could survive in the changing environments and take the place of species that had already been lost.

The fourth team, Team SE, concentrated on water pollution, which includes the chemicals, trash, and air pollution that affect Earth’s bodies of water. Their proposal was to create a giant water filter that would filter out the trash and bad chemicals in the water and properly dispose of them.

All of the teams were very creative with their solutions and had a fun time collaborating to create the best pitch possible. Each of the pitches were very engaging and every speaker got the chance to improve their presentational skills. All of the attendees rated each group’s pitches based on their creativity, delivery, and adherence to the prompt on a google form, and those with the highest score won gift cards. The meeting was a cross between STEM and the business and startup side of the world and the valuable skills that can be applied to both fields.
